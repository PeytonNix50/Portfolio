About Me:

I was born in Augusta, Georgia and raised in Aiken, South Carolina. I moved to Indiana with my parents around 10 years old. I went to Heritage Hills High School and graduated in 2016. After highschool, I attended the University of Southern Indiana. While there I realized that I did not belong at a university. I then withdrew from school and went back home to work as a machine operator at Spencer Industries. After about a year of working at Spencer Industries, I was promoted to a CNC Technician. This position was my first experience with coding. As a CNC Technician, my job was to make sure the CNC mill was cutting to the correct dimensions that were specified in the engineering plan of the specified part we were making. I enjoyed the problem-solving that came along with this job and went on to be a CNC Technician for roughly 2 years. Fast-forward to 2020 and COVID-19 had me laid-off from my job at Spencer Industries. During this time I found the Eleven Fifty Academy and a gracious grant from the state of Indiana to help finance my training at EFA.
